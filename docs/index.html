      Philosophy · not a fight

      Floke vs cloud

      A calm comparison — we build for people who want software on their machine. Cloud tools have their place. This is ours.

      We are not anti-internet — we are pro-ownership. Floke apps sync releases through GitHub, not through a vendor lock-in dashboard. Your library, habits, and chats stay on disk you control.
      Floke drag Cloud
      Core features · local data
      Often limited or read-only
      No dunking on competitors. Just what Floke optimizes for — and what we deliberately don't chase.
      TopicFlokeTypical cloud app
      Works offlineCore features · local dataOften limited or read-only
      PrivacyYour disk · no upload by defaultData on vendor servers
      PriceFree / buy once · no subscriptionMonthly tiers · lock-in
      AccountsOpen what you downloadedSign-in required
      UpdatesGitHub · predictable rhythmSilent changes · A/B tests
      AI / metadataGlyph on-device · optional local LLMCloud inference · training on your data
      OwnershipYou keep files if we disappearAccess tied to subscription

      When cloud wins

      Real-time collaboration across continents, massive shared datasets, and zero-install web demos — cloud is the right tool. Floke does not try to replace that.

      When Floke wins

      Personal libraries, daily habits, local chat, and games you own — software that should work on a plane, in a cabin, or after the Wi‑Fi dies. No permission slip required.

      Studio manifesto

      Privacy

      One policy spirit across Senza, Cultiva, BLIP, Flint, Glyph, and Engine — your machine first.

      Floke does not monetize attention. We do not sell profiles, train ad models on your habits, or require an account to use what you downloaded. Telemetry — when it exists — is opt-in and documented per app.

      Per-app notes

      Each product page includes App Privacy with specifics. This page is the shared promise.

      This website
